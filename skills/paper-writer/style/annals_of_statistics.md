# Style: Annals of Statistics

Position: flagship theory journal. The contribution *is* the theorem;
everything else supports it.

## Section template

- No stated page limit; ~25–35 pages typical (norm). Technical appendices
  may move to an electronic supplement.
- Intro → Setup → Main Results → Proofs (or sketches + supplement) →
  Simulations → Real Data (optional) → Discussion.
- IMS author package (imsart class); author-year citations.

## Tone

- Most formal of the profiled venues. Complete mathematical statements;
  every symbol defined before use; no marketing language at all.
- "We" sparingly; conventional passive fine in technical sections.
- Intuition paragraphs are welcome *after* precise statements, not instead
  of them.

## Reviewer expectations

- Assumption minimality challenged line by line: for each (A1, A2, …),
  when does it fail? Is a weaker version known?
- Sharp rates with explicit constants where possible; minimax lower bounds
  expected when claiming optimality.
- Novelty judged against the classical literature (not just recent ML);
  cite the standard textbooks/monographs for known machinery.
- Identifiability and regularity conditions stated precisely.

## Distinct dos

- State the main theorem early (Main Results section), full proof in the
  paper or supplement — never proof-by-citation for the main result.
- Simulations designed to corroborate specific theorems (coverage, rates),
  not benchmark tables.

## Don'ts

- No numerical-performance framing of a theory contribution.
- No vague "under regularity conditions" — enumerate them.
- No bulleted Related Work or Discussion — flowing prose; enumerate only
  assumptions and algorithm steps (prose_hygiene §F).
